## VTEX

### Company Overview
- **Founded**: 2000 (Rio de Janeiro, Brazil)
- **Ticker**: VTEX (NYSE)
- **Revenue**: ~$220M (FY2024)
- **CEO**: Geraldo Thomaz Jr.
- **Employees**: ~2,000
- **Market position**: #1 enterprise e-commerce platform in LatAm. Composable commerce leader

### Key Products
- **VTEX Commerce Platform**: Unified commerce (marketplace, OMS, CMS, PIM, search)
  - Marketplace: Build your own marketplace or connect to existing ones
  - VTEX IO: Serverless development platform (React + Node.js + GraphQL)
  - VTEX Intelligent Search: AI-powered product search and merchandising
  - Order Management System (OMS): Distributed order orchestration, ship-from-store
  - VTEX Sales App: Clienteling for in-store associates (endless aisle, mobile POS)
  - VTEX Ad Network: Retail media (sponsored products within e-commerce)
  - VTEX Pick and Pack: Fulfillment app for store associates
- **VTEX Shipping Network**: Carrier integration hub for LatAm (Correios, 99, Loggi, etc.)
- **Live Shopping**: Livestream commerce (integrated with VTEX storefront)

### Clients
- AB InBev, Whirlpool, Sony, Motorola, Carrefour, Coca-Cola, Samsung, L'Oreal, Nestlé, Stanley, LEGO, Electrolux

### SMB Relevance
- **Primarily mid-market to enterprise** — not ideal for micro-SMBs (complex, higher cost)
- **VTEX IO** enables agencies to build custom storefronts quickly
- **Marketplace model**: SMBs can sell on VTEX-powered marketplaces (like seller on Amazon model)
- **Pricing**: Revenue share model (typically 1-3% of GMV + platform fee)

### APIs & Integration
- **VTEX APIs**: REST + GraphQL — Catalog, Orders, Checkout, Pricing, Logistics, Payments, CMS, Search
- **VTEX IO**: Build custom apps/extensions (React frontend + Node.js backend)
- **App Store**: Marketplace of pre-built integrations
- **Webhooks**: Order, payment, catalog change notifications

### LatAm: Dominant in Brazil enterprise e-commerce. Strong in Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile. 40+ countries total

## Tiendanube / Nuvemshop

### Company Overview
- **Founded**: 2010 (Buenos Aires, Argentina)
- **Valuation**: ~$3.1B (2022 round) — LatAm's largest e-commerce SaaS platform for SMBs
- **CEO**: Santiago Sosa
- **Employees**: ~1,500
- **Countries**: Argentina, Brazil, Mexico, Colombia, Chile
- **Market position**: "The Shopify of LatAm" — #1 SMB e-commerce platform in the region

### Key Products
- **Online store builder**: Drag-and-drop, templates, custom domains
- **Payments**: Integrated checkout (Mercado Pago, Nuvem Pago in Brazil, cards, bank transfers, cash)
- **Shipping**: Integrated with LatAm carriers (Correo Argentino, Andreani, Mercado Envios, Correios)
- **Marketing**: Instagram/Facebook shop sync, Google Shopping, email marketing, abandoned cart recovery
- **Nuvemshop Envios**: Negotiated shipping rates (discounted through volume)
- **App store**: 100+ integrations (WhatsApp, ERP, accounting, CRM, logistics)
- **Tiendanube for Developers**: Open platform for agencies and devs

### Plans & Pricing (Argentina example)
| Plan | Price | Transaction Fee |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Free (Starter) | Free | 2% per sale |
| Impulso | ~$6,900 ARS/mo | 1.5% |
| Escala | ~$13,900 ARS/mo | 1% |
| Next (enterprise) | Custom | 0.5% |

### SMB Relevance
- **Purpose-built for LatAm SMBs**: Local payment methods, local carriers, local language
- **WhatsApp selling**: Deep integration (product catalog on WhatsApp, order via chat)
- **Instagram shop**: Auto-sync products to Instagram Shopping
- **Free plan**: Start selling with zero upfront cost
- **130,000+ active stores** across LatAm

### APIs
- **Tiendanube API**: REST API — products, orders, customers, categories, scripts, webhooks
- **Nuvemshop Partners**: Agency/developer partner program
- **App SDK**: Build embedded apps for the Tiendanube app store

## Falabella.com (Chile)

### Overview
- **Parent**: Falabella S.A. (Santiago, Chile) — largest department store chain in LatAm
- **Countries**: Chile, Peru, Colombia, Argentina, Mexico, Brazil
- **Revenue**: ~$12B (Falabella group, FY2024)

### Key Products
- **Falabella.com**: Online marketplace + own inventory (like Amazon model)
  - Electronics, fashion, home, sports, beauty, grocery (Tottus)
- **Falabella Marketplace**: Third-party sellers (SMBs can list products)
- **CMR (credit card)**: Falabella's own credit card (massive in Chile/Peru/Colombia)
- **Fpay**: Digital wallet / payment platform
- **Linio** (acquired, now integrated): Was a separate LatAm marketplace, merged into Falabella.com
- **Fazil**: Quick commerce / express delivery

### SMB Relevance
- **Marketplace seller program**: SMBs list on Falabella.com (commission 8-25% depending on category)
- **CMR/Fpay integration**: Access Falabella's financial ecosystem (millions of cardholders)
- **Logistics**: Falabella handles fulfillment for marketplace sellers (FBF — Fulfilled by Falabella)

## 99 Minutos (Mexico)

### Overview
- **Founded**: 2015 (Mexico City, Mexico)
- **Valuation**: ~$200M+ | **Employees**: ~1,500
- **Countries**: Mexico, Colombia, Peru, Chile
- **Market position**: Leading last-mile delivery for e-commerce in Mexico

### Key Products
- **Same-day delivery**: Pick up from merchant, deliver same day (Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey)
- **Next-day delivery**: National coverage in Mexico
- **Fulfillment**: Warehouse + pick-pack-ship services
- **Scheduled delivery**: Customer picks time slot
- **Returns management**: Reverse logistics

### SMB Relevance
- **Affordable same-day delivery**: Starts at ~$49 MXN (~$2.75) per shipment in Mexico City
- **API integration**: Connect your Shopify/Tiendanube/WooCommerce store
- **No minimum volume**: Even small merchants can use the service
- **Dashboard**: Track all shipments, print labels, manage returns

### APIs
- **99 Minutos API**: REST API — create shipments, track packages, manage labels, webhooks
- **Pre-built plugins**: Shopify, WooCommerce, VTEX, Tiendanube

## Skydropx (Mexico)

### Overview
- **Founded**: 2017 (Monterrey, Mexico)
- **Acquired by**: Enviame (Chilean logistics company)
- **Market position**: Multi-carrier shipping platform for LatAm e-commerce

### Key Products
- **Multi-carrier aggregator**: Compare rates across DHL, FedEx, Estafeta, Redpack, 99 Minutos, UPS
- **Single API**: Ship with any carrier through one integration
- **Label generation**: Bulk shipping label creation
- **Tracking**: Unified tracking across all carriers
- **E-commerce plugins**: Shopify, WooCommerce, Tiendanube, VTEX

### SMB Relevance
- **Volume discounts**: SMBs get enterprise-level shipping rates
- **No minimum volume**: Pay per shipment
- **Free plan available**: Up to 25 shipments/month free
- **Dashboard**: Compare carrier prices, choose cheapest/fastest option per shipment

## Amazon LatAm

### Presence
- **Amazon Mexico**: Full marketplace (amazon.com.mx) — fastest-growing Amazon marketplace in LatAm
- **Amazon Brazil**: Full marketplace (amazon.com.br) — FBA, Prime, Alexa
- **Amazon Colombia**: Launched 2023 (marketplace)

- **FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon)**: SMBs ship to Amazon warehouse, Amazon handles fulfillment
- **Amazon Seller Central**: Full marketplace seller tools
- **Amazon Ads**: Sponsored Products, Brands, Display
- **Amazon Lending**: Working capital for sellers (Mexico, Brazil)

## iFood (Brazil)

### Overview
- **Founded**: 2011 (Sao Paulo, Brazil)
- **Valuation**: ~$5.4B | **Employees**: ~5,000
- **Market position**: #1 food delivery in Brazil (80%+ market share). Owned by Prosus (Naspers)

### Key Products
- **Food delivery**: Restaurant to consumer delivery
- **Grocery delivery**: iFood Mercado (supermarket delivery)
- **iFood Beneficios**: Meal voucher card (competing with traditional VR/VA cards)
- **iFood Pago**: Payment solution for restaurants
- **iFood for Restaurants**: Restaurant management tools (POS, analytics, marketing)

### SMB Relevance
- **Restaurant onboarding**: Essential for any restaurant in Brazil
- **iFood Shop**: Enables restaurants to sell directly
- **Commission**: 12-27% (varies by plan — Basic, Entrega, Plus)
- **iFood Ads**: Promote restaurants within the app
