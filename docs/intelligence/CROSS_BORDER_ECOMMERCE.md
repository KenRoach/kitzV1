## 1. Executive Summary

### Latin America E-Commerce: A $191 Billion Opportunity

Latin America is the fastest-growing e-commerce region worldwide. In 2025, retail e-commerce sales surged 12.2% to $191.25 billion, with projections of $215.31 billion in 2026 and a path to $1 trillion by 2027. Argentina, Brazil, and Mexico together account for nearly 85% of all e-commerce sales in the region, and 84% of online purchases are made via mobile devices.

### Panama's Strategic Position

Panama sits at the crossroads of the Americas with unique advantages for cross-border e-commerce:

- **Colon Free Zone (ZLC)** -- The largest free zone in the Western Hemisphere, with record 241 new company installations in H1 2025 and 13.3% growth in commercial activity. Law No. 8 of 2016 specifically promotes e-commerce within the zone.
- **Panama Pacifico** -- A special economic zone hosting 160+ companies including eight Fortune 500 firms (3M, Dell, BASF), with income tax as low as 5% and total exemption on services exported outside Panama.
- **Territorial Tax System** -- Only income sourced within Panama is taxed. Foreign-sourced e-commerce revenue is taxed at 0%.
- **Panama Canal** -- Unmatched logistics connectivity between the Atlantic and Pacific, shortening shipping times to all major LatAm markets.
- **Free Trade Agreements** -- FTAs with the USA, Mexico, Chile, Peru, Colombia, Canada, Costa Rica, Central America, Singapore, South Korea, and EFTA countries.

### Key Legal Challenges

Each destination country has different:
- Consumer protection laws (withdrawal periods range from 0 to 10 days)
- Tax regimes (VAT/IVA from 12% to 21%)
- Customs procedures and de minimis thresholds ($0 to $500)
- Data protection requirements (LGPD, LFPDPPP, etc.)
- Electronic invoicing standards (CFDI, NF-e, Factura Electronica)
- Payment method preferences (Pix, OXXO, PSE, cash)

### Kitz's Opportunity

Kitz can become the operating system that simplifies cross-border compliance for LatAm SMBs by:
1. Automating landed cost calculations (duties + taxes + shipping)
2. Generating compliant invoices for each destination country
3. Enforcing consumer protection rules (withdrawal periods, warranty disclosures)
4. Supporting local payment methods per destination
5. Managing customs documentation and HS code classification
6. Providing country-specific compliance checklists

## 2. Panama as an E-Commerce Origin Hub

### 2.1 Colon Free Zone (Zona Libre de Colon)

**What it is:** The Colon Free Zone (ZLC), established in 1948, is the largest free trade zone in the Western Hemisphere and the second largest in the world after Hong Kong. It is a designated area in the city of Colon on the Caribbean side of the Panama Canal where goods can be imported, stored, modified, and re-exported without paying Panamanian customs duties.

**How SMBs can use it:**
- Import goods from Asia, the USA, or Europe at zero duty
- Store inventory in ZLC warehouses
- Re-export to any LatAm country, leveraging Panama's FTAs for preferential tariff rates
- Use ZLC as a fulfillment hub for cross-border e-commerce orders
- Access e-commerce-specific infrastructure enabled by Law No. 8 of 2016

**Tax advantages:**
- No import duties on goods entering the ZLC
- No export duties on goods leaving the ZLC
- No income tax on operations within the zone (for qualifying activities)
- No ITBMS (Panama's VAT equivalent) on zone transactions
- No capital tax, dividend tax, or complementary tax for ZLC operations

**Requirements to operate:**
- Register a company in Panama (S.A., S.R.L., or branch of foreign company)
- Employ a minimum of 5 Panamanian nationals
- No minimum capital investment required (though typical initial investments are $100,000-$150,000)
- No commercial license required
- Choose an operational structure:
  - Lot Rental Contract: rent land and build your own warehouse
  - Locale Rental Contract: rent existing office/warehouse space ($2-6/sqm for warehouses, $10-20/sqm for showrooms)
  - Operations Permit Contract: $2,400/year for permit-based operations
  - Public Warehouse Services: use shared facilities

**Types of businesses in ZLC:**
- Import/re-export trading companies
- Warehousing and distribution centers
- Light manufacturing and assembly
- E-commerce fulfillment operations
- Financial and commercial services
- Technology companies (since 2016 reforms)

**Re-export model:**
The classic ZLC model involves importing goods from Asia (China, Japan, South Korea) or the USA, storing them in the zone, and re-exporting to Latin American markets. For e-commerce, this model enables:
1. Bulk import at manufacturer prices (no duty)
2. Individual order fulfillment from ZLC inventory
3. Direct shipping to end consumers across LatAm
4. Leveraging Panama's FTAs for reduced tariffs at destination

**Recent growth (2025):**
- 241 new company installations in H1 2025 (record)
- 13.3% rise in commercial activity in March 2025
- 5-6% growth in re-exports vs. 2024
- "New Field" expansion project announced July 2025: 250 hectares, $500 million investment

### 2.2 Panama Pacifico

**What it is:** A special economic zone on the former Howard Air Force Base, focused on high-tech, logistics, and multinational operations. Over 160 companies currently operate there, including 3M, Dell, and BASF.

**Key benefits for tech/e-commerce companies:**
- Reduced income tax rate of 5% (vs. 25% standard Panama rate)
- Total exemption from income tax on services exported outside Panama
- Exemption from dividend tax
- Exemption from ITBMS (VAT)
- Reduced withholding tax of 2%
- Tax-free importation of equipment and materials
- Streamlined residency and work permits for foreign employees
- One-stop shop for government permits and approvals

**Industry focus:**
- High-technology and innovation
- Call centers and shared services
- Logistics and supply chain management
- Sea and air transportation
- E-commerce platform operations

**For Kitz:** Panama Pacifico is an ideal base for a technology company operating an e-commerce platform. Services provided to SMBs outside Panama would be exempt from income tax. The reduced tax environment, combined with proximity to the Panama Canal and Tocumen International Airport, makes it a compelling headquarters option.

### 2.3 Panama's Tax Advantages for Cross-Border

**Territorial tax system:**
Panama operates on a strict territorial tax system. Only income generated from Panamanian sources is subject to local taxation. This means:
- Revenue from sales to customers in Colombia, Brazil, Mexico, etc. = **not taxable in Panama**
- Revenue from providing SaaS services to foreign clients = **not taxable in Panama**
- Revenue from re-exporting goods through the ZLC = **not taxable in Panama** (zone-specific exemptions)
- Only income from domestic Panamanian sales is taxed at the standard 25% corporate rate

**No capital gains tax on foreign income:**
Capital gains, dividends, and interest from foreign sources are not taxed.

**No withholding tax on foreign payments:**
Panama does not impose withholding taxes on payments made to foreign entities for services rendered outside Panama.

**Transfer pricing considerations:**
- Panama's transfer pricing rules are codified in Chapter IX of the Tax Code (Law 33 of 2010, amended by Law 52 of 2012)
- The Arm's Length Principle applies to related-party transactions
- Five methods available: Comparable Uncontrolled Price, Cost Plus, Resale Price, Profit Split, Transaction Net Margin
- Master File documentation may be requested during audits
- For Kitz SMBs: transfer pricing is primarily relevant if the SMB has related entities in different countries (e.g., a Panama ZLC company selling to a related distributor in Colombia)

## 3. E-Commerce Consumer Protection Laws by Country

### 3.1 Mexico

**Governing law:** Ley Federal de Proteccion al Consumidor (LFPC)
**Enforcement agency:** PROFECO (Procuraduria Federal del Consumidor)
**E-commerce standards:** NOM-151-SCFI-2016 (conservation of electronic messages)

**Right of withdrawal (Derecho de Retracto):**
- **5 business days** from receipt of product or signing of contract
- Consumer is entitled to a full refund
- Product must not have been used
- Exceptions: perishable goods, custom-made products, sealed media/software once opened

**NOM-151 compliance:**
- Mandates conservation of electronic messages for digital transactions
- Requires timestamping, digital hashing, and secure archiving
- Messages must be tamper-evident and retrievable for up to 10 years
- Integration with electronic signature technologies required

**PROFECO Distintivo Digital:**
- PROFECO offers a "Distintivo Digital" (Digital Seal) program for e-commerce sites
- Participating businesses must comply with a Code of Ethics for E-Commerce
- Builds consumer trust for cross-border sellers

**Consumer information requirements:**
- Full seller identification (legal name, RFC tax ID, physical address)
- Clear product descriptions with images
- Total price including taxes displayed in MXN
- Shipping costs and delivery times clearly stated
- Return and exchange policy prominently displayed
- Terms and conditions accessible before purchase
- Spanish language required for consumer-facing content

**Data protection:** Ley Federal de Proteccion de Datos Personales en Posesion de los Particulares (LFPDPPP)
- Privacy notice required before data collection
- Consent required for data processing
- Right to access, rectify, cancel, and oppose data processing (ARCO rights)
- Cross-border data transfers permitted with adequate safeguards

### 3.2 Colombia

**Governing law:** Ley 1480 de 2011 (Estatuto del Consumidor)
**Enforcement agency:** SIC (Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio)
**E-commerce regulation:** Decreto 587 de 2016 (payment reversal in e-commerce)
**Recent updates:** Ley 2300 de 2023 (anti-spam and consumer contact regulation)

**Right of withdrawal (Derecho de Retracto):**
- **5 business days** from receipt of the product or signing of the contract
- Applies to all distance sales and non-traditional sales methods
- Full refund required within 30 calendar days
- Exceptions: perishable goods, goods made to specification, sealed audio/video/software once opened, newspapers/periodicals

**Payment reversal (Decreto 587 de 2016):**
- Consumers can request payment reversal within 5 business days of becoming aware of a qualifying issue
- Applies to credit card, debit card, and electronic payment transactions
- Payment processors have 15 business days to process the reversal
- **Important limitation:** Only applies when both seller and payment issuer are domiciled in Colombia -- cross-border applicability is limited

**Warranty (Garantia Legal):**
- Minimum warranty varies by product category (typically 1 year for durable goods)
- Manufacturer or importer is primarily responsible
- Consumer may choose: repair, replacement, or refund

**Consumer information requirements:**
- Seller must provide: legal name, NIT (tax ID), physical address, contact information
- Price must include all taxes (IVA displayed)
- Spanish language required
- Detailed product descriptions
- Clear return and warranty policies

**Data protection:** Ley 1581 de 2012 (Ley de Proteccion de Datos Personales)
- Authorization required before collecting personal data
- Data processing only for stated purposes
- Cross-border transfers require adequate protection levels
- SIC is the supervisory authority

### 3.3 Brazil

**Governing law:** Codigo de Defesa do Consumidor (CDC, Law 8.078/1990)
**E-commerce regulation:** Decreto 7.962/2013 (e-commerce specific rules)
**Digital framework:** Marco Civil da Internet (Law 12.965/2014)
**Data protection:** LGPD (Lei Geral de Protecao de Dados, Law 13.709/2018)

**Right of withdrawal (Direito de Arrependimento):**
- **7 calendar days** from receipt of the product or signing of the contract (Article 49, CDC)
- No justification required from the consumer
- Seller cannot charge penalties or impose conditions for accepting returns
- Full refund including shipping costs, with monetary adjustment
- Consumer must be able to exercise withdrawal through the same channel used for purchase
- Seller must immediately notify payment processor/credit card company to cancel or reverse the charge

**Decreto 7.962/2013 requirements:**
- Clear and visible disclosure of seller's legal name (razao social), CNPJ, and physical address
- Prominent display of product characteristics, price (including all additional charges), delivery conditions, and restrictions
- Adequate and effective means for the consumer to exercise the right of withdrawal
- Immediate confirmation of receipt of withdrawal request
- Summary of the contract must be available before purchase completion

**Warranty (Garantia Legal):**
- 30 days for non-durable goods
- 90 days for durable goods
- These are minimum periods; contractual warranties may extend them

**Data protection (LGPD):**
- **Extraterritorial scope:** LGPD applies to any company offering goods or services to individuals in Brazil, regardless of where the company is located
- Legal bases for processing include consent, legitimate interest, and contract execution
- Data subject rights: access, correction, deletion, portability, information about sharing
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) required for high-risk processing
- ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Protecao de Dados) is the regulatory body
- Cross-border data transfers must meet adequacy requirements
- **For Kitz:** Any SMB selling to Brazilian consumers must comply with LGPD, even from Panama

### 3.4 Argentina

**Governing law:** Ley 24.240 (Defensa del Consumidor), amended by Ley 26.361
**Enforcement agency:** Secretaria de Comercio Interior
**Recent updates:** Boton de Arrepentimiento requirement

**Right of withdrawal (Derecho de Revocacion):**
- **10 calendar days** from receipt of the product or signing of the contract
- Applies to all distance contracts and e-commerce transactions
- Full refund required
- Exceptions: custom goods, perishables, sealed media once opened

**Boton de Arrepentimiento (Regret Button):**
- Argentine law requires all e-commerce platforms to include a clearly visible "regret button"
- Must be easily accessible on the website/app
- One-click withdrawal mechanism
- Suppliers engaging in distance contracts must update their platforms to include this feature
- **For Kitz:** Any storefront selling to Argentine consumers must implement this button

**Warranty:**
- 6 months minimum legal warranty on new goods
- 3 months on used goods
- Manufacturer or importer bears primary responsibility

**Data protection:** Ley 25.326 (Proteccion de Datos Personales)
- Argentina has "adequate" data protection status recognized by the EU
- Consent required for data collection and processing
- Cross-border transfers permitted to countries with adequate protection
- Registration with the National Directorate for Personal Data Protection may be required

### 3.5 Chile

**Governing law:** Ley 19.496 (Proteccion de los Derechos de los Consumidores)
**Enforcement agency:** SERNAC (Servicio Nacional del Consumidor)

**Right of withdrawal (Derecho de Retracto):**
- **10 calendar days** from receipt of the product for online/distance purchases
- Full refund required
- Exceptions: perishable goods, personalized items, sealed software/media

**Warranty (Garantia Legal):**
- 3 months minimum for new products
- Right to choose: repair, replacement, or refund
- Seller is the consumer's first point of contact

**Consumer information requirements:**
- Full seller identification
- Price in CLP (Chilean pesos) including all taxes
- Spanish language required
- Clear description of products/services
- Delivery conditions and timelines

**Data protection:** Ley 19.628 (Proteccion de la Vida Privada), major reform underway
- Chile has been modernizing its data protection framework
- New law modeled on GDPR expected/enacted
- Consent-based processing with expanded data subject rights

### 3.6 Peru

**Governing law:** Codigo de Proteccion y Defensa del Consumidor (Ley 29571)
**Enforcement agency:** INDECOPI (Instituto Nacional de Defensa de la Competencia y de la Proteccion de la Propiedad Intelectual)

**Right of withdrawal:**
- Peru does not have a general statutory right of withdrawal for e-commerce purchases comparable to other LatAm countries
- However, INDECOPI enforces "idoneidad" (suitability): products must match their descriptions
- Sellers must honor their published return policies
- Defective products must be repaired, replaced, or refunded

**Warranty:**
- Products must be fit for their intended purpose
- Seller and manufacturer share responsibility
- INDECOPI handles consumer complaints and can impose sanctions

**Data protection:** Ley 29733 (Ley de Proteccion de Datos Personales)
- Consent required for data processing
- Data Protection Authority: Direccion General de Proteccion de Datos Personales
- Cross-border transfers require adequate protection or consent

### 3.7 Costa Rica

**Governing law:** Ley de Promocion de la Competencia y Defensa Efectiva del Consumidor (Ley 7472)
**Enforcement agency:** Ministerio de Economia, Industria y Comercio (MEIC)

**Right of withdrawal:**
- Costa Rica provides a right of withdrawal for certain distance purchases
- Specific period and conditions are set out in implementing regulations
- Consumer must return the product in original condition

**Warranty:**
- Seller must guarantee products are free from defects
- Consumer right to repair, replacement, or refund

**Data protection:** Ley de Proteccion de la Persona Frente al Tratamiento de sus Datos Personales (Ley 8968)
- Comprehensive data protection framework
- PRODHAB (Agencia de Proteccion de Datos de los Habitantes) is the regulatory body

### 3.8 Ecuador

**Governing law:** Ley Organica de Defensa del Consumidor
**Enforcement agency:** Defensorias del Consumidor

**Right of withdrawal:**
- Consumers may withdraw from distance purchases
- Products must be returned in original condition
- Specific periods are governed by implementing regulations

**Warranty:**
- Legal warranty required on all products
- Defective products must be repaired or replaced

**Data protection:** Ley Organica de Proteccion de Datos Personales (LOPDP, 2021)
- Modeled on GDPR
- Comprehensive data subject rights
- Cross-border transfer restrictions apply

### 3.9 Dominican Republic

**Governing law:** Ley 358-05 (Proteccion de los Derechos del Consumidor o Usuario)
**Enforcement agency:** Pro Consumidor

**Consumer protection:**
- General consumer protection framework covering e-commerce
- Products must match descriptions and be fit for purpose
- Clear pricing requirements
- Return/exchange obligations for defective products

**Data protection:** Ley 172-13 (Proteccion Integral de los Datos Personales)
- Data protection framework in place
- Consent required for processing

### 3.10 United States

**Governing law:** Federal Trade Commission Act, various federal and state laws
**Enforcement agencies:** FTC (Federal Trade Commission), state attorneys general
**Key regulations:** CAN-SPAM Act, COPPA, INFORM Consumers Act, FTC Mail Order Rule

**Right of withdrawal:**
- No federal mandatory cooling-off period for online purchases
- FTC "Cooling-Off Rule" (3 days) applies only to door-to-door sales, not online
- Individual sellers' return policies govern (and must be honored)
- Many states have consumer protection statutes with varying requirements

**FTC e-commerce requirements (2025):**
- **INFORM Consumers Act:** Online marketplaces must collect, verify, and disclose information about high-volume third-party sellers (FTC brought first enforcement action against Temu in September 2025)
- **Rule on Unfair or Deceptive Fees:** Effective May 12, 2025, prohibits bait-and-switch pricing and hidden fees
- **Consumer Review Rule:** Prohibits fake reviews, buying reviews, insider reviews without disclosure, and review suppression
- **Mail Order Rule:** Retailers must ship within the timeframe advertised, or within 30 days if no timeframe specified
- **CAN-SPAM Rule:** Commercial email compliance requirements
- **COPPA:** Protection of children's data online (under 13)

**Warranty:**
- Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act governs written warranties
- UCC implied warranties (merchantability, fitness for purpose) apply unless disclaimed
- FTC Guides on warranties require clear disclosure

**Data protection:** No single federal privacy law; patchwork of state laws
- California: CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act/Rights Act)
- Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, and others have enacted privacy laws
- Sector-specific: HIPAA (health), GLBA (financial), COPPA (children)

### 3.11 Canada

**Governing law:** Competition Act (federal), provincial consumer protection statutes
**Enforcement agency:** Competition Bureau (federal), provincial authorities
**Anti-spam:** CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation)

**Right of withdrawal:**
- Provincial laws govern; varies by province
- Ontario: 7 days for "internet agreements" over $50
- Other provinces have similar provisions

**CASL requirements:**
- Express consent required before sending commercial electronic messages
- Unsubscribe mechanism required
- Sender identification required
- Significant penalties for non-compliance (up to $10M per violation for businesses)

**Data protection:** PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
- Consent-based framework
- "Adequacy" status with the EU
- Provincial privacy laws in Alberta, British Columbia, and Quebec
- Quebec's Law 25 (2023) introduced GDPR-like requirements

## 4. Cross-Border Customs & Import Regulations

### 4.1 De Minimis Thresholds by Country (2025-2026)

The de minimis threshold is the shipment value below which imports may enter a country with reduced or no customs duties and/or taxes. These thresholds are critical for e-commerce economics.

| Country | Duty De Minimis | Tax De Minimis | Notes | Last Updated |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mexico** | $50 USD | $50 USD | Simplified procedure for express shipments up to $117 for US-origin goods; RFC required for all imports since Oct 2024 | 2025 |
| **Colombia** | $200 USD | $0 (tax applies to all) | $200 threshold for personal imports; formal declaration above $2,000 | 2025 |
| **Brazil** | $50 USD (with conditions) | $0 (17% ICMS applies to all) | Under Remessa Conforme: 20% duty under $50, 60% duty over $50; all shipments subject to 17% ICMS | 2025 |
| **Argentina** | $0 | $0 | No de minimis. All imports subject to duties and taxes regardless of value. Import licensing eliminated April 2025. | 2025 |
| **Chile** | $500 USD (per item) | $0 (19% VAT on all imports) | Updated Oct 25, 2025. Raised from $41 to $500 for customs duty. VAT applies to all imports regardless of value. | Oct 2025 |
| **Peru** | $200 USD (CIF) | $200 USD (CIF) | Below $200 CIF: exempt from duties and taxes. Above $2,000: formal declaration required with customs agent. | 2025 |
| **Costa Rica** | $0 | $0 | No de minimis threshold. All imports subject to duties and taxes. | 2025 |
| **Ecuador** | $400 USD | $400 USD | Weight limit of 400kg also applies | 2025 |
| **Dominican Republic** | ~$17 USD | ~$17 USD | DOP 1,000 threshold | 2025 |
| **USA** | **$0 (as of Aug 29, 2025)** | **$0** | De minimis ELIMINATED. Previously $800. All imports now require formal/informal entry with duties, taxes, and fees. China/HK excluded since May 2, 2025. | Aug 2025 |
| **Canada** | $150 CAD (duty, suspended) | $40 CAD (tax, suspended) | CUSMA rates for US/Mexico origin courier shipments. **Suspended as of July 1, 2025 for 24 months** as a "calibration measure." | Jul 2025 |

**Critical observations for Kitz:**
1. The trend is toward lower or zero de minimis thresholds globally
2. The US elimination of the $800 threshold (Aug 2025) is a major shift affecting all cross-border e-commerce
3. Argentina and Costa Rica have no de minimis at all -- every shipment is taxed
4. Chile's recent increase to $500 per item is a rare positive development
5. Brazil's Remessa Conforme program creates a structured compliance pathway but still imposes duties on all imports

### 4.2 Import Duties and Taxes

**How destination country VAT/IVA applies to imports:**

| Country | VAT/IVA Rate | Applied To | Collection Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mexico | 16% | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs or by platform |
| Colombia | 19% | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |
| Brazil | 17% ICMS | CIF value + duty | Collected at purchase (Remessa Conforme) or at customs |
| Argentina | 21% | CIF value + duty + statistical fee | Collected at customs |
| Chile | 19% | CIF value + duty | Collected at purchase (Simplified Regime) or at customs |
| Peru | 18% IGV | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |
| Costa Rica | 13% | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |
| Ecuador | 12-15% | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |
| Dominican Republic | 18% ITBIS | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |
| USA | No federal sales tax | State/local sales tax varies (0-10.25%) | Complex nexus rules |
| Canada | 5% GST + provincial | CIF value + duty | Collected at customs |

**DDP vs DDU -- Who pays?**

| Model | Full Name | Who Pays | Experience | Kitz Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DDP** | Delivered Duty Paid | Seller pays all duties, taxes, and fees | Buyer sees one total price, no surprises at delivery | Recommended for premium experience and markets with low de minimis |
| **DDU/DAP** | Delivered Duty Unpaid / Delivered at Place | Buyer pays duties, taxes, and fees at delivery | Buyer faces unexpected charges, risk of refusal | Acceptable for high-de-minimis markets or when DDP logistics are unavailable |

**Kitz recommendation:** Default to DDP for the best customer experience. Calculate and display the total "landed cost" at checkout using the formula:

```
Landed Cost = Product Price + Shipping + Insurance + Import Duty + Destination VAT/IVA + Customs Processing Fee
```

### 4.4 E-Commerce Customs Simplification Programs

**Brazil -- Remessa Conforme (PRC):**
- Voluntary compliance program effective since August 2023
- Open to national and foreign e-commerce companies
- PRC-registered companies benefit from reduced 20% import duty on shipments up to $50 USD (CIF)
- Standardized 17% ICMS collected by the platform at time of sale
- Shipments over $50: 60% import duty with $20 deduction
- Companies must collect duties and ICMS from customers before shipping
- Must inform customers about product origin and total value including taxes
- Provides faster customs clearance

**Mexico -- Simplified Customs for E-Commerce:**
- Express shipments under $50 USD: tax and duty free
- Shipments $50-$117 USD (US-origin under USMCA): simplified procedure available
- As of August 15, 2025: 33.5% global rate for non-USMCA entries above threshold
- 19% rate for USMCA-origin shipments over $117 meeting conditions
- RFC (Mexican Tax ID) required for all import declarations since January 2025
- Manifestacion de Valor (Value Declaration) mandatory since December 9, 2025

**Colombia -- Postal Simplification:**
- Personal imports under $200 USD via postal/courier: simplified declaration
- Larger shipments require formal customs declaration
- 4x4 program for frequent importers (limited)

**Chile -- Simplified Taxation Regime (Oct 2025):**
- International platforms can register to collect VAT upfront

## 5. Cross-Border Payment & Tax Obligations

### 5.1 VAT/IVA on Cross-Border Sales

**Origin vs. Destination Principle:**
Cross-border e-commerce is generally taxed at the **destination** -- meaning the buyer's country's VAT/IVA rate applies, not Panama's. Since Panama's ITBMS (7%) typically does not apply to exports, the key question is whether the seller must register for and collect the destination country's VAT.

**VAT registration requirements for foreign sellers:**

| Country | Registration Required? | When? | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mexico | Yes, for digital services | If selling digital goods/services to Mexican consumers | Direct registration with SAT |

| Brazil | Complex | Remessa Conforme for physical goods; digital services subject to various taxes | Platform-based collection for PRC; otherwise at customs |
| Argentina | Not directly (withholding) | WHT on cross-border payments covers the obligation | 31.5% WHT on digital service payments (17.5% for certain media) |
| Chile | Yes, for digital services | If selling digital services to Chilean consumers | Registration with SII (Simplified Regime) |
| Peru | Proposed/recent | IGV on digital services from December 2024 | Registration pathway being established |
| USA | No federal requirement | State nexus rules for sales tax | Economic nexus thresholds vary by state |

### 5.5 Transfer Pricing

**When transfer pricing rules apply:**
- When a Panama-based SMB sells to a related entity in another country (e.g., a subsidiary, branch, or affiliated distributor)
- Common scenario: Panama ZLC company sells goods at a markup to a related distribution company in Colombia

**Documentation requirements:**
- Most LatAm countries require transfer pricing studies and documentation
- Master File requirement is mandatory in Mexico, Brazil, Chile, Argentina, Colombia, Peru, Ecuador, and Uruguay
- Panama requires documentation under Law 33/2010 and may request it during audits

**For SMBs:** Transfer pricing is typically relevant only for businesses with related-party cross-border transactions. Simple arm's length pricing (selling to unrelated consumers at market prices) does not trigger transfer pricing concerns. Kitz should flag this when an SMB sets up entities in multiple countries.

## 8. Social Commerce & WhatsApp Commerce Regulations

### 8.1 Social Commerce Legal Framework

**Is selling via WhatsApp/Instagram legally valid?**
Yes. In all LatAm jurisdictions, contracts formed through electronic means (including messaging apps) are generally legally valid. Key legal frameworks supporting this:

- **Panama:** Ley 51 de 2008 (Comercio Electronico)
- **Mexico:** Codigo de Comercio (reformed 2003) -- electronic signatures and digital certificates recognized
- **Colombia:** Ley 527 de 1999 (Comercio Electronico) -- data messages have legal equivalence to written documents
- **Brazil:** Provisional Decree 2,200-2/2001 (ICP-Brasil) -- electronic signatures legally valid
- **Peru:** Ley 27269 (Ley de Firmas Digitales y Certificados) -- electronic signatures equivalent to handwritten
- **Chile:** Ley 19.799 (Firma Electronica) -- electronic signatures recognized
- **Ecuador:** Ley de Comercio Electronico (2002) -- electronic signatures and data messages regulated

**Requirements for social commerce transactions:**
1. **Proof of purchase:** The seller must provide a receipt or invoice, even for WhatsApp transactions
2. **Consumer information:** All mandatory pre-purchase disclosures must be made (price, product description, seller identification, return policy)
3. **Record keeping:** Conversation logs serve as evidence of contract formation
4. **Consumer protection:** All consumer protection laws (including withdrawal rights) apply equally to social commerce
5. **Data protection:** Privacy notices and consent requirements apply to customer data collected via WhatsApp

### 8.2 WhatsApp Commerce Compliance

**Meta Commerce Policy requirements:**
- Businesses must comply with WhatsApp Business Terms of Service and the Meta Commerce Policy
- Prohibited: selling weapons, drugs, tobacco, adult content, real currency, or other restricted items
- Products must have accurate descriptions and pricing
- Businesses are solely responsible for taxes, duties, and fees
- Non-compliance may result in permanent account suspension

**WhatsApp Business API for Kitz:**
- Use WhatsApp Business API (not the consumer app) for commercial communications
- Implement catalog features for product display
- Support payment links (linking to Kitz checkout)
- Automated order confirmations and shipping notifications
- Template messages for post-purchase communication

**Cross-border WhatsApp commerce considerations:**
- WhatsApp numbers must be verified per Meta's requirements
- Different countries may have different messaging regulations (e.g., CASL in Canada, anti-spam in Colombia under Ley 2300 de 2023)
- Payment collection via WhatsApp should redirect to secure payment links, not collect card details in chat
- Transaction records must be maintained for tax and consumer protection compliance

```typescript
interface LandedCostBreakdown {

  productPriceCurrency: CurrencyCode;

  PE: 0.18,  CR: 0.13,  EC: 0.15,  DO: 0.18,  US: 0.00,
  CA: 0.05,  GT: 0.12,  HN: 0.15,  SV: 0.13,  NI: 0.15,
  UY: 0.22,  PY: 0.10,

function calculateLandedCost(
  productPrice: number,
  shippingCost: number,
  destination: DestinationCountry,
  hsCode: HSCodeInfo,
  insuranceRate: number = 0.01, // 1% of product price
  hasFTACertificate: boolean = false,
): LandedCostBreakdown {
  const notes: string[] = [];

  // Step 1: Calculate CIF value
  const insuranceCost = productPrice * insuranceRate;
  const cifValue = productPrice + shippingCost + insuranceCost;

  // Step 2: Determine applicable duty rate
  const deMinimis = DE_MINIMIS_THRESHOLDS[destination];
  let importDutyRate: number;
  let deMinimisApplied = false;
  let freeTradeAgreementApplied = false;

  if (cifValue <= deMinimis.dutyThreshold && deMinimis.dutyThreshold > 0) {
    importDutyRate = 0;
    deMinimisApplied = true;
    notes.push(`De minimis applied: CIF $${cifValue} <= threshold $${deMinimis.dutyThreshold}`);
  } else if (hasFTACertificate && hsCode.ftaDutyRate < hsCode.generalDutyRate) {
    importDutyRate = hsCode.ftaDutyRate;
    freeTradeAgreementApplied = true;
    notes.push(`FTA preferential rate applied: ${hsCode.ftaDutyRate * 100}% vs ${hsCode.generalDutyRate * 100}% MFN`);
  } else {
    importDutyRate = hsCode.generalDutyRate;
    notes.push(`MFN duty rate applied: ${hsCode.generalDutyRate * 100}%`);
  }

  // Special handling for Brazil (Remessa Conforme)
  if (destination === 'BR') {
    if (cifValue <= 50) {
      importDutyRate = 0.20; // 20% under Remessa Conforme
      notes.push('Brazil Remessa Conforme: 20% duty for shipments under $50 CIF');
    } else {
      importDutyRate = 0.60; // 60% for over $50
      notes.push('Brazil: 60% duty for shipments over $50 CIF (with $20 deduction)');
    }
  }

  // Step 3: Calculate import duty
  let importDutyAmount = cifValue * importDutyRate;

  // Brazil $20 deduction for shipments over $50
  if (destination === 'BR' && cifValue > 50) {
    importDutyAmount = Math.max(0, importDutyAmount - 20);
    notes.push('Brazil: $20 deduction applied to duty');
  }

  // Step 4: Calculate destination VAT
  const vatRate = VAT_RATES[destination];
  let vatBase = cifValue + importDutyAmount;
  let vatAmount: number;

  if (cifValue <= deMinimis.taxThreshold && deMinimis.taxThreshold > 0) {
    vatAmount = 0;
    notes.push(`Tax de minimis applied: CIF $${cifValue} <= threshold $${deMinimis.taxThreshold}`);
  } else {
    vatAmount = vatBase * vatRate;
    notes.push(`Destination VAT: ${vatRate * 100}% on $${vatBase.toFixed(2)}`);
  }

  // Step 5: Customs processing fees (estimated)
  const customsProcessingFee = destination === 'US' ? 6.75 : // US MPF minimum
    destination === 'CA' ? 9.95 : // CBSA handling
    destination === 'BR' ? 3.50 : // Brazil postal handling
    2.00; // Generic estimate

  // Step 6: Calculate total landed cost
  const totalLandedCost = productPrice + shippingCost + insuranceCost +
    importDutyAmount + vatAmount + customsProcessingFee;

  return {
    productPrice,
    productPriceCurrency: 'USD',
    shippingCost,
    insuranceCost,
    cifValue,
    importDutyRate,
    importDutyAmount,
    vatRate,
    vatAmount,
    customsProcessingFee,
    otherFees: 0,
    totalLandedCost,
    totalLandedCostLocal: totalLandedCost, // Would multiply by exchange rate
    exchangeRate: 1.0, // Would fetch live rate
    freeTradeAgreementApplied,
    deMinimisApplied,
    notes,
  };
}
```

```typescript
// ============================================================
// COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS ENGINE
// ============================================================

interface ComplianceChecklist {

interface ComplianceItem {
  category: ComplianceCategory;
  requirement: string;
  status: 'met' | 'not_met' | 'not_applicable' | 'needs_review';
  priority: 'critical' | 'high' | 'medium' | 'low';
  details: string;
  actionRequired?: string;
}

type ComplianceCategory =
  | 'consumer_protection'
  | 'tax_registration'
  | 'invoicing'
  | 'customs_documentation'
  | 'returns_policy'
  | 'data_protection'
  | 'product_labeling'
  | 'payment_processing'
  | 'language_localization'
  | 'shipping_logistics';

function getComplianceRequirements(
  destination: DestinationCountry,
  productType: 'physical' | 'digital' | 'service',
  merchantConfig: MerchantConfig,
): ComplianceChecklist {
  const rules = CONSUMER_PROTECTION_RULES[destination];
  const items: ComplianceItem[] = [];
  const criticalIssues: string[] = [];
  const warnings: string[] = [];

  // 1. Consumer Protection: Withdrawal Period
  if (rules.withdrawalPeriodDays > 0) {
    const hasWithdrawalPolicy = merchantConfig.returnPolicy?.withdrawalDays >= rules.withdrawalPeriodDays;
    items.push({
      category: 'consumer_protection',
      requirement: `${rules.withdrawalPeriodDays} ${rules.withdrawalType} day withdrawal period`,
      status: hasWithdrawalPolicy ? 'met' : 'not_met',
      priority: 'critical',
      details: `${getCountryName(destination)} requires a ${rules.withdrawalPeriodDays}-${rules.withdrawalType}-day withdrawal period for e-commerce purchases.`,
      actionRequired: hasWithdrawalPolicy ? undefined :
        `Update return policy to allow ${rules.withdrawalPeriodDays} ${rules.withdrawalType} day withdrawals`,
    });
    if (!hasWithdrawalPolicy) {
      criticalIssues.push(`Withdrawal period does not meet ${getCountryName(destination)} requirements`);
    }
  }

  // 2. Argentina: Regret Button
  if (rules.requiresRegretButton) {
    const hasRegretButton = merchantConfig.storefront?.hasRegretButton ?? false;
    items.push({
      category: 'consumer_protection',
      requirement: 'Boton de Arrepentimiento (Regret Button)',
      status: hasRegretButton ? 'met' : 'not_met',
      priority: 'critical',
      details: 'Argentine law requires a clearly visible "regret button" on all e-commerce platforms.',
      actionRequired: hasRegretButton ? undefined :
        'Implement a visible regret/withdrawal button on the storefront',
    });
    if (!hasRegretButton) {
      criticalIssues.push('Missing required Boton de Arrepentimiento for Argentina');
    }
  }

  // 3. Tax Registration
  const requiresRegistration = ['MX', 'CO', 'CL', 'PE', 'CA'].includes(destination);
  if (requiresRegistration && productType === 'digital') {
    items.push({
      category: 'tax_registration',
      requirement: `VAT/IVA registration for digital services in ${getCountryName(destination)}`,
      status: 'needs_review',
      priority: 'critical',
      details: `Selling digital services to ${getCountryName(destination)} may require VAT registration with the local tax authority.`,
      actionRequired: 'Consult with tax advisor regarding registration requirements',
    });
    warnings.push(`VAT registration may be required for digital services sold to ${getCountryName(destination)}`);
  }

  // 4. Invoicing Format
  if (rules.invoiceFormat !== 'generic_invoice' && rules.invoiceFormat !== 'none') {
    items.push({
      category: 'invoicing',
      requirement: `${rules.invoiceFormat.toUpperCase()} compliant invoicing`,
      status: merchantConfig.invoicing?.format === rules.invoiceFormat ? 'met' : 'not_met',
      priority: 'high',
      details: `${getCountryName(destination)} requires invoices in ${rules.invoiceFormat} format.`,
      actionRequired: `Configure invoice generation for ${rules.invoiceFormat} format`,
    });
  }

  // 5. Language Localization
  if (rules.requiresSpanishContent || rules.requiresPortugueseContent || rules.requiresBilingualContent) {
    const langs: string[] = [];
    if (rules.requiresSpanishContent) langs.push('Spanish');
    if (rules.requiresPortugueseContent) langs.push('Portuguese');
    if (rules.requiresBilingualContent) langs.push('English + French');

    items.push({
      category: 'language_localization',
      requirement: `Content in ${langs.join(', ')}`,
      status: 'needs_review',
      priority: 'high',
      details: `Consumer-facing content must be available in ${langs.join(' and ')}.`,
      actionRequired: `Ensure product listings, terms, and policies are translated to ${langs.join(', ')}`,
    });
  }

  // 6. Data Protection
  items.push({
    category: 'data_protection',
    requirement: `${rules.dataProtectionLaw} compliance`,
    status: 'needs_review',
    priority: 'high',
    details: `Customer data processing must comply with ${rules.dataProtectionLaw}.`,
    actionRequired: 'Review privacy policy and data handling practices for compliance',
  });

  // 7. Customs Documentation (physical goods only)
  if (productType === 'physical') {
    items.push({
      category: 'customs_documentation',
      requirement: 'Commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin',
      status: 'needs_review',
      priority: 'critical',
      details: 'Physical goods require proper customs documentation for import clearance.',
      actionRequired: 'Configure automated customs document generation',
    });
  }

  // 8. Product Labeling (physical goods only)
  if (productType === 'physical') {
    items.push({
      category: 'product_labeling',
      requirement: `Product labeling per ${getCountryName(destination)} requirements`,
      status: 'needs_review',
      priority: 'medium',
      details: `Products must meet ${getCountryName(destination)} labeling requirements including language, origin, ingredients/materials.`,
      actionRequired: 'Review product labels against destination country requirements',
    });
  }

  // 9. Payment Processing
  items.push({
    category: 'payment_processing',
    requirement: `Local payment methods for ${getCountryName(destination)}`,
    status: 'needs_review',
    priority: 'high',
    details: `Supporting local payment methods significantly improves conversion rates in ${getCountryName(destination)}.`,
    actionRequired: 'Configure local payment method support via dLocal/EBANX',
  });

  // Calculate overall status
  const criticalNotMet = items.filter(i => i.priority === 'critical' && i.status === 'not_met').length;
  const anyNotMet = items.filter(i => i.status === 'not_met').length;
  const overallStatus = criticalNotMet > 0 ? 'not_compliant' :
    anyNotMet > 0 ? 'partially_compliant' : 'compliant';

  return {
    country: destination,
    countryName: getCountryName(destination),
    items,
    overallStatus,
    criticalIssues,
    warnings,
  };
}

    GT: 'Guatemala', HN: 'Honduras', SV: 'El Salvador',

}
```

### 12.3 Strategic Recommendations for Kitz

1. **Start with USD economies:** Ecuador and El Salvador eliminate FX risk entirely. Costa Rica and Dominican Republic are close enough geographically and regulatorily to bundle with Tier 1.

2. **Build compliance infrastructure incrementally:** Each new market adds compliance requirements. Build a modular system where country-specific rules are pluggable:
   - Withdrawal period engine (configurable per country)
   - Invoice format adaptors (CFDI, NF-e, DTE, etc.)
   - Tax calculation modules (per destination)
   - Payment method integrations (via dLocal/EBANX)

3. **Invest heavily in Brazil compliance early:** Despite being Tier 4 in complexity, Brazil represents 33%+ of LatAm e-commerce. The Remessa Conforme program provides a clear compliance pathway. Start building NF-e, Pix, LGPD, and Portuguese localization capabilities from day one.

4. **Leverage Panama's FTA network:** The fact that Panama has FTAs with nearly every major LatAm market is a significant differentiator. Build FTA certificate of origin generation into the platform to automatically apply preferential tariff rates.

5. **Default to DDP (Delivered Duty Paid):** The customer experience of receiving unexpected customs charges at delivery is a conversion killer. Calculate and collect all duties and taxes at checkout.

6. **Partner for e-invoicing:** Rather than building CFDI, NF-e, and Factura Electronica capabilities in-house, partner with specialized providers (e.g., Sovos, Avalara, or local PAC/certification providers in each country).

7. **Monitor de minimis thresholds continuously:** These thresholds are changing rapidly (US elimination, Chile increase, Canada suspension). Build an alerting system for threshold changes that affect merchant pricing and margins.

8. **Implement the "Regret Button" as a platform feature:** Since Argentina requires it and other countries may follow, make a withdrawal/cancellation button a standard Kitz storefront feature, configurable per destination.
