## 1. Regional GDP & Growth

### Headline Numbers

| Metric | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 |
|--------|------|------|------|
| LatAm GDP growth | 2.4% | 2.2-2.4% | 2.3% |
| South America | 2.0% | 2.9% | 2.4% |
| Central America | 3.5% | 2.6% | 3.0% |
| Caribbean | 2.8% | 3.1% | 3.2% |

Source: ECLAC, OECD, World Bank (2025-2026 projections)

### Key Dynamics
- Four consecutive years of ~2.3% growth — a "prolonged period of low growth" (ECLAC)
- Labour productivity growing only 0.9% annually (1991-2024) vs 1.2% OECD average
- Employment growth slowing: 2.0% (2024) → 1.5% (2025) → 1.3% (2026)
- Persistent poverty and inequality reflecting weak job creation
- Inflation moderating across most countries but still above pre-pandemic levels

### Structural Challenges
- Low productivity = biggest barrier to sustained growth
- Limited fiscal space — most governments running deficits
- High informality = weak tax base
- Climate vulnerability increasing economic shocks
- Dependence on commodity exports (copper, oil, soybeans, coffee)

### Bright Spots
- Argentina recovering from 2024 recession
- Central America outperforming South America in growth
- Digital transformation accelerating productivity
- Services sector expanding (especially tech-enabled)
- Remittances providing economic floor ($170B+ annually)

## 3. SMB & Micro-Enterprise Landscape

### The Numbers That Define Kitz's Market

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| SMEs as % of all LatAm businesses | 99.5% | OECD 2024 |
| Microenterprises as % of all firms | 88% | OECD 2024 |
| SME share of formal employment | 60% | ECLAC |
| SMEs operating informally | ~50% | OECD 2024 |
| Solo sellers as % of small businesses | 88% | PCMI 2024 |
| Informal workers in LatAm | 63% | ILO |
| Informal economy as % of GDP | 33% | World Bank |
| SMBs with access to formal credit | <40% | OECD 2024 |
| SMBs receiving institutional financing | 15% | Multiple |

### The Credit Gap
- SMEs generate 50-60% of GDP but receive only 15% of institutional financing
- Over 60% of SMBs face barriers to formal credit
- Lack of credit histories and tangible assets = excluded from traditional banking
- Alternative finance (P2P platforms) is often the ONLY option
- Embedded credit (R2, Kapital) emerging as solution

### SMB Pain Points (What Kitz Solves)
1. **No integrated tools** — managing via WhatsApp + notebook + Excel
2. **No credit access** — can't prove revenue to banks
3. **No automation** — everything manual, owner-dependent
4. **No digital presence** — selling only via word-of-mouth and social
5. **No compliance help** — formalization is confusing and expensive
6. **No data** — decisions made on gut, not metrics

### The Solo Seller Economy
- 88% of all small businesses are solo sellers
- Primary channel: WhatsApp + Instagram + Facebook Marketplace
- Average daily time on WhatsApp: 38 minutes
- Most don't consider themselves "businesses" — just hustling
- Biggest barrier: transitioning from informal to formal
- Biggest opportunity: giving them business infrastructure they didn't know they needed

## 13. Informal Economy Deep-Dive

### The Scale
- 63% of LatAm workers are informal
- 50% of businesses operate informally
- 33% of GDP is informal
- Mexico: 8.5M informal small businesses (63% of total)
- Cash >50% of spending in Guatemala, Dominican Republic

### Why Informality Persists
1. **Tax burden** — formal registration = immediate tax obligations
2. **Bureaucracy** — registration takes weeks/months in many countries
3. **Cost** — legal, accounting, licensing fees
4. **No perceived benefit** — informal businesses can't access credit anyway
5. **Cultural norm** — "everyone does it this way"
6. **Lack of tools** — no affordable business infrastructure

### The Formalization Opportunity
- Governments incentivizing through digital payment requirements
- Pix (Brazil) making informal transactions visible
- Mobile wallets creating financial footprint for unbanked
- Embedded credit enabling lending without traditional credit scores
- Digital invoicing mandates spreading across countries

### Kitz's Role in Formalization
- Provide business infrastructure BEFORE requiring formalization
- Track sales, customers, orders in the system = build credit history
- Generate invoices, receipts = create paper trail
- Compliance guidance per country = reduce fear of formalization
- Value prop: "Formalize because it benefits you, not because someone forces you"
