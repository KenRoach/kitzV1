- **Priority:** `CRITICAL`
- **ID:** PKB-509
- **URL:** https://paymentscmi.com/insights/main-payment-methods-latin-america-brazil-mexico-chile-peru-colombia-argentina/
- **Type:** Guide
- **Why KITZ Needs It:** Comprehensive 2025 guide: dominant payment methods per country, trends, market shares
- **Fetch Status:** OK (8000 chars)

**Extracted Intelligence:**

PCMI (Payments and Commerce Market Intelligence) is the leading payments intelligence firm globally, with outstanding research in Latin American e-commerce payment methods.

**Key Macro Trends:**
- 35 million new internet consumers in LATAM between 2024-2026 (approaching full penetration)
- 340% growth in fintech companies between 2017-2024 (Mastercard/AMI 2025 study)
- Significant decrease in cash for both online and offline transactions
- Declining use of credit/debit cards
- Increase in digital wallets and Real-Time Payment (RTP) systems
- LATAM has the highest RTP growth projection globally (29% CAGR 2022-2027, ACI Worldwide)

**LATAM E-Commerce Payment Mix (2024):**
- **Credit Cards:** 42% market share (down from 56% in 2019 -- 14 percentage point decline in 5 years)
- **PIX (Brazil only):** 22% of all LATAM e-commerce payments
- **Digital Wallets + A2A:** 46% of e-commerce turnover (LATAM has highest A2A share globally thanks to PIX)
- Alternative methods gaining rapidly: Nequi, Daviplata (Colombia), Yape, PLIN (Peru), MODO (Argentina), SPEI (Mexico), SINPE Movil (Costa Rica), Simple (Bolivia)

**In-Person Payments (Worldpay 2024 data):**
- Cash: 25% of in-person sales volume (projected to drop to 17% by 2030)
- Credit cards: 25% (tied with cash)
- Digital wallets: Expected to reach 23% of in-person sales by 2030 (+10 pp from 2024)
- Two wallet types: tokenized (Apple Pay, Google Pay) and balance-based (store money, link bank accounts)

**Country-by-Country Breakdown:**

**Brazil E-Commerce:**
- Credit cards: 44% share
- PIX: 40% share (projected to surpass credit cards by 2027 at 51% vs 36%)
- PIX at POS: 33% of total payment volume
- Cash: Dropped from 68% of POS in 2014 to 17% in 2024 (PIX adoption)

**Mexico E-Commerce:**
- Debit cards: 38% share (2024)
- PCMI projects debit cards to reach 46% by 2027
- Credit cards growing at 12% CAGR (2024-2027)
- Debit cards growing at 32% CAGR
- Digital methods experiencing strongest growth

**Colombia, Argentina, Chile, Peru:** Detailed breakdowns available in source. Key trends include Nequi dominance in Colombia, MODO growth in Argentina, and Yape prevalence in Peru.

**KITZ Action Items:**
- PIX integration is non-negotiable for Brazil
- Support for local A2A methods (PSE, Nequi, SPEI, Yape) is critical for pan-LATAM expansion
- Credit card processing still essential but declining in share
- Cash voucher methods (Boleto, OXXO) still relevant for unbanked population
- Digital wallet acceptance accelerating at point of sale
