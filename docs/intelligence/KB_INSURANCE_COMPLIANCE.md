## Small Business Insurance Essentials

### Why SMBs Need Insurance
- **Legal requirement** — Most countries mandate certain coverages
- **Asset protection** — Protect business from lawsuits and losses
- **Customer trust** — Insured businesses are more credible
- **Contract requirements** — Many clients and platforms require proof of insurance
- **Peace of mind** — Focus on growth, not risk

### Core Coverage Types

#### 1. General Liability Insurance (CGL) [Critical]
- **What it covers:** Third-party bodily injury, property damage, personal/advertising injury
- **Who needs it:** Every business that interacts with customers or public
- **Typical limits:** $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate
- **Cost:** $300-$1,500/year for small businesses
- **LATAM context:** Required for commercial spaces, events, client-facing operations

#### 2. Professional Liability / E&O (Errors & Omissions) [High]
- **What it covers:** Claims of negligence, mistakes, or failure to deliver services
- **Who needs it:** Service businesses, consultants, tech companies, agencies
- **Typical limits:** $1M-$5M
- **KITZ relevance:** Recommended for KITZ users offering professional services

#### 3. Workers' Compensation [Critical where applicable]
- **What it covers:** Employee injuries, medical expenses, lost wages
- **Required by law** in most countries
- **LATAM specifics:**
  - **Panama:** CSS (Caja de Seguro Social) covers workplace injuries — employer contributions mandatory
  - **Mexico:** IMSS (Instituto Mexicano del Seguro Social) — employer must register and contribute
  - **Brazil:** INSS — employer contributions based on risk category
  - **Colombia:** ARL (Administradora de Riesgos Laborales) — mandatory for all employees
  - **Argentina:** ART (Aseguradora de Riesgos del Trabajo) — mandatory workplace risk insurance

#### 4. Property Insurance [High]
- **What it covers:** Physical assets — equipment, inventory, office space
- **Who needs it:** Businesses with physical locations or valuable equipment
- **Consider:** Fire, theft, natural disasters, vandalism

#### 5. Cyber Liability Insurance [High - Growing]
- **What it covers:** Data breaches, ransomware, customer data compromise
- **Why critical:** 92% of companies in Mexico have been cyberattack victims
- **KITZ relevance:** Essential for businesses handling customer payment data
- **Typical cost:** $500-$5,000/year depending on data volume

#### 6. Business Interruption Insurance [Medium]
- **What it covers:** Lost income when business can't operate (disaster, pandemic, etc.)
- **Important for:** Businesses dependent on physical location or supply chain

## Paperwork Best Practices

### Insurance Documentation Checklist
- [ ] **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** — Keep current certificates readily available
- [ ] **Policy declarations page** — Summary of coverage limits and deductibles
- [ ] **Endorsements** — Additional coverages or modifications to base policy
- [ ] **Claims history** — Record of all past claims (affects premiums)
- [ ] **Premium payment receipts** — Proof of active coverage
- [ ] **Agent/broker contact info** — Accessible for emergencies

### Annual Insurance Review SOP
1. **January** — Review all policies for renewal dates
2. **February** — Assess business changes (new products, employees, locations)
3. **March** — Request quotes from 3+ providers
4. **April** — Compare coverage and premiums
5. **Before renewal** — Update coverage to match current business needs
6. **After renewal** — Update COIs, notify clients/partners
7. **Quarterly** — Verify all premiums are current

### Records Retention
- **Active policies** — Keep for duration + 7 years after expiry
- **Claims files** — Keep indefinitely
- **COIs** — Keep current + 5 years archived
- **Premium receipts** — Keep for 7 years (tax deductible)

## LATAM-Specific Insurance Requirements by Country

### Panama
- **CSS contributions** — Employer: 12.25% of payroll, Employee: 9.75%
- **Workplace safety** — Risk insurance through CSS
- **Commercial license** — Municipal operating permit (Aviso de Operación)
- **Vehicle insurance** — Mandatory for business vehicles

### Mexico
- **IMSS** — Mandatory social security (employer 20-35% of payroll depending on risk)
- **SAT compliance** — Insurance premiums are tax deductible
- **CFDI** — Insurance invoices must be electronic (CFDI format)
- **Minimum coverage** — Workers' comp, property if leasing commercial space

### Brazil
- **INSS** — Social security contributions (employer ~20% + RAT risk adjustment)
- **FGTS** — 8% payroll to employee severance fund
- **Work accident insurance** — Based on risk rating (1-3%)
- **D&O insurance** — Growing requirement for company directors

### Colombia
- **ARL** — Mandatory workplace risk insurance (0.348% to 8.7% of payroll based on risk)
- **EPS** — Health insurance (employer 8.5%, employee 4%)
- **AFP** — Pension (employer 12%, employee 4%)
- **SOAT** — Mandatory vehicle accident insurance

### Argentina
- **ART** — Mandatory workplace risk insurance
- **Obra Social** — Health coverage through union-affiliated plans
- **Insurance premiums** — Deductible from income tax (Ganancias)

## Best Practices for KITZ Users

### Before Buying Insurance
1. List all business assets (physical + digital)
2. Identify your biggest risks (customer injury, data breach, employee injury)
3. Check local legal requirements for your industry
4. Get quotes from 3+ providers
5. Understand deductibles vs premiums tradeoffs

### Digital Insurance Management
- Scan and upload all documents (cloud backup)
- Set calendar reminders for renewal dates (60 days before)
- Track premium payments in accounting software
- Keep broker on speed dial

### Cost-Saving Tips
- **Bundle policies** — Package discounts for multiple coverage types
- **Higher deductibles** — Lower premiums if you can absorb small losses
- **Risk reduction** — Safety measures, training, and compliance reduce premiums
- **Pay annually** — Often 5-15% cheaper than monthly payments
- **Industry associations** — Group rates through trade organizations
