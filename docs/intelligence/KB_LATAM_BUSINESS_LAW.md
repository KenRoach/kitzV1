## Panama

### Commercial Law
- **Commercial Code**: Código de Comercio (Law 2 of 1916, amended)
- **Business Registration**: Registro Público de Panamá — all companies must register
- **Commercial License**: Aviso de Operación via Panamá Emprende (free, online)
- **Corporate Types**: SA (most common), SRL, branch of foreign company
- **Foreign Investment**: No restrictions on foreign ownership — 100% foreign-owned OK
- **Free Trade Zones**: Colón Free Zone, Panama Pacífico, City of Knowledge

### Labor Law
- **Labor Code**: Código de Trabajo (Law 44 of 1995, amended)
- **Minimum Wage**: Varies by region and industry ($350-$800/month)
- **Working Hours**: 48 hrs/week max (8 hrs/day, 6 days)
- **Overtime**: 25% premium (daytime), 50% (nighttime), 75% (holidays)
- **13th Month (Décimo Tercer Mes)**: Mandatory — paid in 3 installments (April 15, Aug 15, Dec 15)
- **Vacation**: 30 days per year after 1 year of service
- **Severance**: Based on years of service — 3.4 weeks per year
- **Social Security (CSS)**: Employer 12.25%, Employee 9.75%
- **2025 Reform**: Draft law proposes revoking Article 212 — would end no-cause dismissal for employees <2 years
- **Probation**: 3 months for general workers, up to 1 year for domestic workers

### Key for KITZ Users
- No personal income tax on foreign-sourced income
- Territorial tax system — only Panama-sourced income taxed
- ITBMS (7% VAT) applies to most goods and services
- Free zones offer tax incentives for export businesses

## Mexico

### Commercial Law
- **Commercial Code**: Código de Comercio (1889, heavily amended)
- **Business Formation**: SAS (online, free, <MX$5M), SA de CV, S de RL
- **Foreign Investment Law**: Allows 100% foreign ownership in most sectors
- **E-Commerce**: NOM-151-SCFI-2002 regulates electronic commerce
- **Consumer Protection**: PROFECO (Federal Consumer Protection Agency)
- **IP**: IMPI (Mexican Institute of Industrial Property) for trademarks/patents

### Labor Law
- **Federal Labor Law (LFT)**: Ley Federal del Trabajo (1970, reformed 2019, 2024)
- **Minimum Wage 2026**: 13% increase effective Jan 1, 2026 (MX$278.80/day general)
- **Working Hours**: 48 hrs/week max (6 days × 8 hrs)
- **40-Hour Workweek**: Bill reintroduced — possible approval 2026, enforcement 2027
- **Overtime**: 100% premium first 9 hrs/week, 200% beyond
- **Vacation**: 12 days after 1 year, increases 2 days/year up to 20, then +2 every 5 years
- **Vacation Premium**: 25% of vacation pay
- **Christmas Bonus (Aguinaldo)**: 15 days salary minimum (Dec 20 deadline)
- **Profit Sharing (PTU)**: 10% of pre-tax profits distributed to employees
- **Social Security (IMSS)**: Employer 20-35% depending on risk category
- **Severance**: 3 months salary + 20 days/year of service + seniority premium
- **Remote Work Law**: Employers must cover internet and proportional electricity costs

### Key for KITZ Users
- CFDI electronic invoicing mandatory — SAT compliance critical
- RESICO simplified regime for businesses <MX$3.5M/year
- Profit sharing (PTU) can be significant cost — plan for it

## Brazil

### Commercial Law
- **Civil Code**: Código Civil (Law 10,406/2002) — governs business entities
- **Commercial Registry**: Junta Comercial (state-level)
- **Business Types**: MEI, ME, EPP, Ltda, SA, EIRELI
- **Consumer Protection**: CDC (Código de Defesa do Consumidor, Law 8,078/1990)
- **E-Commerce**: Decree 7,962/2013 regulates online commerce
- **Data Protection**: LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados, Law 13,709/2018) — Brazil's GDPR
- **Foreign Investment**: Generally open, some sector restrictions

### Labor Law
- **CLT (Consolidação das Leis do Trabalho)**: Law-Decree 5,452/1943 — comprehensive labor code
- **Minimum Wage 2026**: R$1,518/month (expected increase)
- **Working Hours**: 44 hrs/week (8 hrs/day + 4 hrs Saturday) or flexible schedules
- **Overtime**: 50% premium minimum (100% on Sundays/holidays)
- **13th Salary**: Mandatory — paid in 2 installments (Nov + Dec)
- **Vacation**: 30 days after 12 months, +1/3 vacation bonus
- **FGTS**: 8% of salary to employee severance fund (employer pays)
- **INSS**: Employee 7.5-14% (progressive), Employer ~20% + RAT (1-3%)
- **Severance**: 40% FGTS penalty on dismissal without cause
- **2025 Reform**: Telework proposal for temporary workers under CLT framework
- **Meal/Transport Vouchers**: Common benefits, tax-deductible for employer

### Key for KITZ Users
- Simples Nacional is the best regime for small businesses (unified tax)
- MEI is the simplest structure (solo, <R$81K/year, one employee max)
- LGPD compliance required for any data collection
- NF-e electronic invoicing mandatory

## Colombia

### Commercial Law
- **Commercial Code**: Código de Comercio (Decree 410 of 1971, amended)
- **SAS Law**: Law 1258 of 2008 — Simplified Stock Company (most popular for SMBs)
- **Consumer Protection**: Law 1480 of 2011 (Estatuto del Consumidor)
- **E-Commerce**: Law 527 of 1999 (electronic commerce)
- **Data Protection**: Law 1581 of 2012 (Habeas Data) — enforced by SIC
- **Foreign Investment**: Generally open, must register with Banco de la República

### Labor Law
- **Substantive Labor Code**: Código Sustantivo del Trabajo (1950, amended)
- **Minimum Wage 2026**: ~COP $1,500,000/month + transportation allowance
- **Working Hours**: 42 hrs/week (2026 — gradually reducing from 48 to 42 by 2026)
- **Overtime**: 25% daytime, 75% nighttime, 100% holidays
- **Severance (Cesantías)**: 1 month salary per year, deposited to fund by Feb 14
- **Interest on Severance**: 12% annual, paid to employee by Jan 31
- **Vacation**: 15 business days per year
- **Prima de Servicios**: 1 month salary (half in June, half in December)
- **Health (EPS)**: Employer 8.5%, Employee 4%
- **Pension (AFP)**: Employer 12%, Employee 4%
- **Workplace Risk (ARL)**: Employer 0.348%-8.7% based on risk level

### Key for KITZ Users
- SAS is the easiest and cheapest to form (COP $200K-$500K)
- Electronic invoicing mandatory via DIAN
- Colombia has strong consumer protection — honor warranties

## Argentina

### Commercial Law
- **Civil & Commercial Code**: Unified code (Law 26,994 of 2014)
- **Business Types**: SAS, SRL, SA, Monotributista
- **SAS Law**: Law 27,349 of 2017 — online 24-hour formation
- **Consumer Protection**: Law 24,240 (Defensa del Consumidor)
- **Data Protection**: Law 25,326 (Personal Data Protection)
- **Foreign Investment**: Open but currency controls (cepo cambiario) complicate operations
- **2025 Reform**: Fines removed for registration defects, digital pay slips accepted

### Labor Law
- **Labor Contract Law (LCT)**: Law 20,744 of 1974 (heavily amended)
- **Minimum Wage (SMVM)**: Adjusted monthly/quarterly due to inflation
- **Working Hours**: 48 hrs/week (8 hrs/day)
- **Overtime**: 50% weekdays, 100% weekends/holidays
- **SAC (Aguinaldo)**: 1 month salary/year, paid in 2 installments (June 30, Dec 18)
- **Vacation**: 14 days (<5 years), 21 days (5-10 years), 28 days (10-20 years), 35 days (20+ years)
- **Severance**: 1 month per year of service (capped at best 3 months salary)
- **ART (Workplace Risk)**: Mandatory insurance, employer-paid
- **Obra Social**: Health insurance through union-affiliated plans
- **Monotributo**: Simplified all-in-one monthly payment for qualifying small businesses

### Key for KITZ Users
- Monotributo simplifies everything for solo/small operations
- Currency controls affect international transactions — plan accordingly
- Inflation means constant price adjustments needed
- Argentina has strong labor protections — dismissal costs are high

## Chile

### Commercial Law
- **Commercial Code**: Código de Comercio (1865, amended)
- **Business Types**: SpA (most popular), EIRL, Ltda, SA
- **Online Registration**: Tu Empresa en un Día (1-day company formation via registroempresas.cl)
- **Consumer Protection**: SERNAC (Servicio Nacional del Consumidor)
- **Data Protection**: Law 19,628 (updated 2024 with stricter provisions)
- **Foreign Investment**: Fully open, national treatment

### Labor Law
- **Labor Code**: Código del Trabajo (DFL 1, 2002, amended)
- **Minimum Wage**: ~CLP $500,000/month (2026)
- **Working Hours**: 42 hrs/week (2026 — reduced from 45; going to 40 by 2028)
- **Overtime**: 50% premium, max 2 hrs/day
- **Vacation**: 15 business days per year
- **Gratificación**: Profit sharing — 25% of net profit (capped at 4.75 monthly salaries)
- **Severance (Indemnización)**: 1 month per year, max 11 years (330 days)
- **AFP**: Employee 10% + commission to pension fund
- **Health (Isapre/Fonasa)**: Employee 7%
- **Work-From-Home Law**: Law 21,220 regulates remote work

### Key for KITZ Users
- SpA is the fastest/cheapest structure (online, 1 day)
- Workweek reducing to 40 hrs by 2028 — plan staffing accordingly
- Electronic invoicing via SII mandatory
- Chile is the most business-friendly in LATAM (World Bank rankings)

## Regional Labor Law Trends (2025-2026)

### Key Themes
1. **Shorter workweeks** — Chile (45→40), Colombia (48→42), Mexico (48→40 proposed)
2. **Remote work regulation** — Brazil, Chile, Argentina formalizing WFH rules
3. **Platform/gig worker protection** — Growing regulation of app-based workers
4. **Digital signatures & e-payslips** — Argentina, Mexico accepting digital employment records
5. **Stronger employee protections** — Trend toward harder/costlier dismissals
6. **Data protection** — LGPD (Brazil), LFPDPPP reform (Mexico), Law 81 (Panama)

### Implications for KITZ Users
- **Budget for labor costs** — Social security + benefits = 30-50% on top of base salary
- **13th month/Aguinaldo** — Mandatory in every country, plan cash flow
- **Severance reserves** — Build reserves for potential dismissal costs
- **Contract everything** — Written employment contracts required everywhere
- **Compliance varies by country** — Don't assume one country's rules apply to another
